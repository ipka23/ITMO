Templates and Examples

To tailor the Rational Unified Process for a specific organizational context additional, configuration specific templates are required to reflect exactly how the artifacts, and reports, are to be structured, used and presented.

For some artifacts, and reports, multiple templates may be required to reflect different styles, development tools and view points within the organization.  

The configuration specific templates are organized into sets corresponding to the disciplines.  Several artifacts are used in a number of disciplines (e.g. the Design Guidelines). The templates are presented under the discipline where they are initially instantiated. 

To make the templates accessible and easy to use examples are often required.  These are documented alongside the templates that they follow. 

The configuration specific templates complement, and specialize, the generic templates provided by the Unified Process.  These generic artifact templates are found in the Rational Unified Process browser along with their associated artifacts.  For details of these templates see RUP: Templates.
